Probe-answer cheat sheet

If they askYou answer (short, confident)
"How is this different from Certora / Runtime Verification?" "Formal verification needs humans to write specifications per protocol. Jelleo's hyp library generates checks autonomously per-commit. Complement, not replacement — FV proves once with a human; Jelleo runs continuously without one."
"How is this different from Hypernative / Riverguard / Range / Sec3 X-Ray?" "They watch transactions at runtime — alert when something exploit-shaped lands on-chain. Jelleo watches invariants in source on every commit. Catches bugs before they ship. Different layers."
"How autonomous is it really?" "Hunt cycle is fully autonomous — recon, debate, PoC scaffolding, Kani harness, triage. A human reviews the triaged-confirmed findings before disclosure. That's the only loop."
"What's your team?" "Solo founder right now. Funding scales the team — first hire is a senior auditor for cross-protocol propagation."
"What's the customer model?" "Two types: DeFi insurance protocols who buy attestation feeds for underwriting, and Solana Foundation through STRIDE. Three subscription tiers per protocol — entry around $30K/month, ceiling at $120K/month. The ceiling tier is the number shown on the customer dashboard preview on the site."
"Have you been audited?" "Open-source — every commit public, every check transparent. No external audit on Jelleo itself yet; planning one in year-one. Credibility today is F7 plus the public disclosure track record."
"How are you funded?" "Closing pre-seed now. Proposal in Anatoly's office. Plan is 18-month runway to scale 1 → 27 protocols."
"How much / what terms?" "$1.32M at $10M post, staged with a 90-day Tranche 2 trigger." (Don't elaborate further unless they ask twice.)
"What if Anatoly passes?" "Then I look for the right partner. Methodology already worked once — F7 is the receipt — and the engine's running. Funding scales it; doesn't prove it."
"What do you want from us / from OtterSec?" "Genuinely open question. Possibilities: continuous coverage layer for your audit clients between engagements, a triage tool your auditors use during engagements, joint research / Foundation-funded grant work, something I'm not thinking of. What's your read?"
Anything you don't know "Great question — let me think. What's your read?"
The golden move: "What's your read?" is your best friend. It flips ANY question back to them and admits gaps without weakness. Use it freely. Audit firms love being asked their read — it's the language of their world.
