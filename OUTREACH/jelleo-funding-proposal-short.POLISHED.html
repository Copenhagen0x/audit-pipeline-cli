Jelleo is the underwriting layer for Solana DeFi. We produce continuous, on-chain, cryptographically-signed attestations of code-level invariant integrity that insurance protocols, partner DeFi protocols, and STRIDE evaluators all consume as a live signal. Built on the methodology that produced F7. Today: working v0.1, 125-hypothesis library, 3 autonomously-generated Rust tests passing cargo test, and one disclosed engine-class bug (PR #39) against Anatoly's Percolator.

PR #39 was closed without merging the proposed vault-debit patch; the maintainer absorbed the class into existing engine defenses + adopted A1-class regression coverage labeled PR39/F7 on main at a1afd2e. Honest read: regression-locked coverage on main, not a structural patch.

Today, no insurer offers invariant-grounded, code-anchored coverage tied to live Solana protocol state. Cross-chain coverage exists (Nexus Mutual, InsurAce, Bridge Mutual, plus Solana-native AmuShield by Amulet) — mostly oracle-parametric or audit-anchored; none expose continuous commit-anchored invariant attestation as the underwriting primitive. The smart-contract-integrity surface — the F7 class of bug, where engine math, conservation invariants, and protocol-state transitions are the attack vector — has no continuous code-grounded attestation a counterparty can subscribe to. Jelleo opens the signal layer for invariant-violation parametrics: "as of commit SHA X, all critical invariants verified, signed by Jelleo, slot Y, tx Z." That attestation becomes the underwriting primitive for the integrity surface. Capital is the structural bottleneck for DeFi insurance overall (academic and industry consensus: insurance is capital-constrained, not signal-constrained); Jelleo provides the signal layer, capital partners (crypto-native underwriters or traditional reinsurance) provide balance sheet.

Two-sided market. Protocols pay to get attested (so they can unlock cheaper insurance for their users + present the artifact for STRIDE's smart-contract integrity pillar). DeFi underwriters — Nexus Mutual, Sherlock, AmuShield — are the structural consumer for the registry: they write parametric coverage when a live security signal exists. Audit firms (OtterSec, Asymmetric, Zellic, Neodyme, Halborn, Trail of Bits) deliver $60K–$200K point-in-time engagements; FV incumbents (Certora, Runtime Verification) operate one tier deeper at higher cost. Jelleo is structurally different: continuous code-grounded depth as the base unit, consumed as a live signal. Continuous-detection plays adjacent to Jelleo — several already SF-funded — include Hypernative, Riverguard (Neodyme's separate runtime-monitoring product), Range Security, and Sec3 X-Ray; they operate at runtime / perimeter / static-analysis surfaces and are structurally complementary to Jelleo's commit-anchored invariant attestation, not competing.

What we deliver vs what we report. Reports (24h / weekly / monthly PDFs) document findings. Pillar 3 fix bundles deliver the actual remediation — bug + agent-generated fix + Kani proof + tests, opened as a single PR. Pillar 4 attestations are the consumable signal — published on Solana mainnet, queryable by any program, the foundational primitive insurers and partner protocols ingest.

Continuous on-chain attestation of integrity is the primitive Jelleo proposes to build — no widely-adopted equivalent exists on Solana today, and it's the primitive an underwriter needs to write parametric coverage. 02 · Methodology

Jelleo is the F7 discovery methodology, codified

Jelleo is the disciplined methodology that produced F7, codified into autonomous infrastructure. Not service-hours. Funding scales the same pipeline to ceiling-depth, depth compounding cycle-over-cycle.

The 4-step pipeline

01

Invariant catalog

Each protocol gets a tailored hypothesis library. Today: 125 hand-curated invariants across 10 surface families. Every entry is a falsifiable claim with file/line targets and severity, not prose.

02

Tool-using verification

Each hypothesis dispatched to a Claude agent with read_file, grep, find_function. Agent walks code paths and renders line-cited verdict. No speculation — every claim anchored to file:line.

03

Empirical confirmation

Verdicts needing empirical proof escalate to autonomous Rust test generation. Agent writes a custom cargo test against engine helpers, compiles, runs, classifies PASS/FAIL. Three public in examples/confirmed-tests/.

04

Sibling derivation

Confirmed bugs auto-generate 3–5 sibling hypotheses targeting the same class through different code paths; each goes back into the verification loop. F7's 12-hyp sibling pack came from this. The library compounds — every confirmed finding sharpens the next cycle.

03 · Learning loop

Depth compounds with every cycle

Hypothesis library grows fast — generation throughput observed at ~50 hyps/hour during the F7 sibling-derivation pass (subject to model availability and content density), so a 1,500-hyp library is on disk by month 1. 1,500 is the steady-state cap per protocol (Percolator in Year 1); the cluster catalog scales linearly with each protocol added in Year 2+ — ~3,000–8,000 hyps total across the cluster as multi-protocol coverage rolls out. 04 · Architecture

Four pillars · the autonomous immune system

Four interlocking pillars. Each ships in the 12-month engagement, demonstrated on Percolator. No platform today combines all four.

P1

Counterfactual mainnet detection

Per-tx parallel simulation; state divergence flags the tx within seconds of slot finality. v0.1: shadow (commit-triggered). Funded delta: live mainnet ingestion, sub-slot replay, on-call alert routing.

P2

Cross-protocol bug-class propagation

When a bug is disclosed anywhere, Jelleo auto-extracts the structural pattern and searches every indexed protocol — vulnerable instances flagged in minutes. v0.1: propagate, learn-from-disclosures. Funded delta: multi-protocol indexing + disclosure-feed integrations.

P3

Closed-loop fix bundle

Confirmed bug → agent fix → Kani proves preservation in the bug's neighborhood → tests → single PR. v0.1: confirm, synth-kani scaffold. Funded delta: end-to-end MVP at the 90-day Tranche 2 trigger.

P4

On-chain attestation registry

Every cycle publishes a signed Merkle root: which invariants were verified at which commit SHA — on-chain, queryable, composable. v0.1: no on-chain scaffold (Ed25519 signing exists off-chain). Funded delta: ~500-LOC Anchor program on devnet day 90, mainnet day 180 post-audit.

07 · Revenue model & path to self-funding

How protocols pay, how Jelleo pays for itself

This section answers: how does Jelleo make money, when does it pay for itself, and what does the SAFE convert to?

Jelleo is sold as continuous coverage agreements — protocols contract to run Jelleo against their codebase month over month, billed cost-plus per engagement. Same engagement model audit firms use ($60K–$200K per audit, quote-based) — but with continuous, code-grounded, attestation-backed depth and pricing anchored to measured compute cost.

Pricing — 2.4× revenue markup on raw API cost = 58% gross margin

Engagement price = 2.4× our raw API cost, yielding 58% gross margin. The markup covers methodology IP, on-call disclosure response, Kani fix-bundle delivery, on-chain attestation publishing, and founder/team time. Transparent to protocols, no hidden fee.

Three depth tiers, three customer profiles. Foundation (~300/day Sonnet-only) — lighter compute, daily Sonnet runs without Opus or Kani synthesis, for smaller protocols that want monitoring without the deep-audit overhead. Production ⭐ (500/day Sonnet + tool-using Opus + weekly Kani) — the default for $1B+ TVL DeFi protocols. Ceiling (1,500+/day with multi-round refinement + Kani on every Critical/High) — intended for post-incident hardening, highest-stakes protocols, and protocols where Jelleo's attestation directly unlocks insurance coverage. Customers move between tiers month-over-month; engagement length is open-ended.

Cost-blend is sensitive to caching + batch utilization. Effective per-hyp cost depends on cache-hit rate (0.1× base input on prompt-cache, applied per-protocol on reusable engine context; cache misses cost 1.25× base input as the write-tier surcharge), batch-API utilization (50% off on non-urgent cycles), and the share of Opus + Kani-synthesis turns reserved for highest-value hyps. Per-hyp blended derivations under the assumed mix (~70% cache hit / 50% batch on non-urgent cycles): Foundation ~$1.39/hyp ($12.5K ÷ 300/day Sonnet-only), Production ~$1.67/hyp ($25K ÷ 500/day with Opus share), Ceiling ~$1.10/hyp at the median, $1.55/hyp worst-case ($50K median / $70K worst-case ÷ 1,500/day). The §09 API line is sized at the upper end ($625K total = ~$52K/mo avg, including a $100K worst-case headroom over the $525K $50K-baseline run-rate) so cache-miss-heavy months don't compress engagement margin.

Why Production's per-hyp rate is higher than Ceiling's median. Production runs fewer hyps per day (500 vs 1,500) so a larger share carries Opus + Kani-synthesis turns; Ceiling amortizes those expensive turns across 3× more hyps with cheaper Sonnet single-shot + cache hits carrying the bulk. Foundation sits between the two on per-hyp cost (Sonnet-only is cheap, but lower volume means weaker per-protocol cache amortization).

$1B+ TVL Solana protocols currently spend $150K–$500K/yr on fragmented audits + retainers + bounties (Sherlock 2026). Jelleo Production at $720K/yr replaces that with continuous depth + attestation-backed artifacts. STRIDE Tier-2 ($100M+ TVL) Foundation formal-verification mandate is a potential reimbursement path — protocols qualifying for SF-funded FV may direct that funding toward Jelleo as the artifact-producing layer (no formal partnership today; Year 1 OKR per §01).

Provider-agnostic LLM abstraction. The pipeline runs on Anthropic Sonnet/Opus today; the abstraction layer supports fallback (OpenAI, Google, open-weights) if pricing or rate-limit conditions shift — vendor lock-in is hedged at the abstraction, not the model.

Pricing evolution (Y2+): cost-plus is the structural floor. Year 2+ introduces value-anchored premium pricing for protocols where attestation directly unlocks economic value (e.g. 0.05% of TVL when Jelleo attestation enables an insurance underwriting discount). As API costs decline, cost basis drops with them and margin is preserved on the cost-plus floor.

12 · Strategic fit

Why this engagement is structured this way

  1. Percolator-first, ecosystem-second. Platform built on Percolator source; every safety attestation references engine line numbers. 12-month scope is exclusively Percolator. Multi-protocol expansion is Year 2+ upside — equity story for Series A, not part of the 12-month commitment.
  2. Continuous between audits. Audit firms produce one-shot engagements that stale on the next commit. Jelleo runs 24/7 against upstream — continuous, not point-in-time.
  3. Capital-efficient. Solo operator, AI-native execution. $1.32M = 12 months continuous Percolator coverage + 4 pillars shipped. Equivalent surface area via traditional bi-weekly audits ($60K–$200K each) = $1.5M–$5M for periodic PDFs only. Jelleo: less spend, structurally different deliverables.
  4. Solo-founder concentration risk is acknowledged + mitigated. Solo execution is the capital-efficiency lever AND the largest single risk. Continuity plan (full version in §11): all source + library + harnesses under permissive licenses, Ed25519 signing keys under multi-sig escrow with a named successor reviewer at close, 30-day disclosure pause + Anatoly notification clause if founder is unavailable >7 days, all engagement state in the documented SQLite findings DB. First hire (P4 Anchor + on-chain) lands at month 9 from operating cash flow, not the SAFE.
  5. Inaugural reference. Percolator is the engagement that proves the model. F7 + every finding going forward traces back to this work.
  6. Head-start = compounding library + first-mover + consumer-side switching cost. (a) library, not methodology — every confirmed disclosure adds sibling hyps tied to that protocol's attack surface; late entrants start from zero. (b) first-mover anchor — F7 + Percolator give the longest continuous attestation history on Solana. (c) switching cost — once an underwriter is writing coverage against the registry, swapping providers is a multi-quarter lift. The 12-month funding window converts head-start into Series A timing.
