Verify this cycle offline

The Merkle root is reproducible from the leaves committed in this sidecar. Anyone can recompute the root and verify the platform signature against the public Ed25519 key. No Jelleo service required.

# Fetch this cycle's merkle sidecar
curl -s https://api.jelleo.com/cycles/<root>.merkle.json > cycle.json

# Recompute the root from leaves and verify the signature
audit-pipeline merkle verify --file cycle.json \
  --pubkey https://jelleo.com/keys/jelleo.ed25519.pub

What's in the Merkle leaves

Each leaf is a SHA-256 hash of one canonicalized object. The cycle leaf hashes the cycle's metadata fields; each finding contributes one additional leaf hashing its public fields (finding_id, severity, protocol, poc_fired, engine_sha, wrapper_sha).

The schema is versioned. If the hashed fields ever change, SCHEMA_VERSION increments — historical roots remain reproducible against the schema they were committed under.

Finding content?

By design, this public page does not display per-finding titles, file:line citations, attack-chain verdicts, or PoC paths. That data could telegraph zero-days to attackers if surfaced before disclosure. Customers see their own confirmed findings on the authenticated customer portal; the public attestation here only proves that a finding was sealed in the cycle, not what the finding was.

Once a finding is disclosed (PR opened upstream, embargo lifted, etc.), its title and bug-class appear in the public snapshot.json feed. Until then, the cryptographic count + root here is the public record.
